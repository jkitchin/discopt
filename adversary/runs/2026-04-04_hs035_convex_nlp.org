Same root cause as the Markowitz QP: the QP problem classifier drops
cross-terms (x1*x2, x1*x3). The diagonal-only objective at the solution
point [1.55, 1.05, 0.2] is -3.45, matching exactly. The full objective
at the same point is 0.425.

* Cross-Validation

Direct numpy evaluation of the full objective at discopt's solution gives
0.425, not -3.45. The discrepancy equals exactly the sum of the dropped
cross-terms: 2*1.55*1.05 + 2*1.55*0.2 = 3.875.

* Performance Comparison

All backends report identical results (QP fast path). No regression.

* Verdict

SOLVER_BUG. Same root cause as the Markowitz QP: the QP problem classifier
drops off-diagonal Hessian entries (cross-terms) when extracting the
quadratic form from the expression DAG.
