MPAX is a JAX-native first-order LP solver developed by the MIT Lu Lab
citep:mpax-2024, designed as a drop-in replacement for simplex or interior-
point LP solvers in settings where GPU acceleration, batched solves, or
differentiability matter more than the last few digits of solution
accuracy. For discopt, MPAX is the single most architecturally-aligned LP
backend candidate because it lives natively in the same JAX compute graph
as discopt's relaxation construction, automatic differentiation, and NLP
evaluator.

Issue #37 on the discopt repository proposes MPAX integration as a short-
term win. This article records MPAX's design, its strengths and weaknesses
relative to HiGHS and SCIP's LP stacks, and its role in discopt's near- and
long-term plans.

** Algorithmic Background: PDLP

MPAX is a JAX port of the algorithm introduced in citet:applegate-pdlp-2021
as **PDLP** (Primal-Dual Linear Programming), itself a carefully engineered
instance of primal-dual hybrid gradient (PDHG) applied to LP. The core loop
is a two-line gradient update on the primal and dual variables, wrapped in
a suite of practical enhancements:

- /Adaptive restart/ — periodically reset the iterates to accelerate
  convergence in regions where the objective has flattened.
- /Adaptive step size/ — line search on the primal-dual step sizes to
  balance progress.
- /Diagonal preconditioning/ — Ruiz-style or Pock-Chambolle scaling.
- /Infeasibility detection/ — certificates of primal or dual infeasibility
  from the iterate trajectory.

Per-iteration cost is one sparse matrix-vector product against the
constraint matrix and its transpose; accuracy is typically $10^{-4}$ to
$10^{-6}$ relative, sufficient for B&B bound evaluation and for feasibility
heuristic guidance, not sufficient for termination-proof LP bounds in
exact-arithmetic settings.

citet:lu-cupdlp-2023 demonstrated a GPU implementation (cuPDLP.jl) that
solves LPs with $10^8$+ nonzeros competitively with multi-node CPU simplex
on a single GPU. MPAX brings the same design to the JAX ecosystem.

** Differentiators of MPAX over cuPDLP

- **JAX-native**: MPAX is written entirely in JAX primitives, so the solver
  is =jit=-compiled to XLA, =vmap=-able over batches of LP instances, and
  in principle differentiable through the optimum via implicit function
  theorems. cuPDLP.jl is Julia; cuPDLP-C is C/CUDA. Neither composes with
  discopt's existing JAX DAG.
- **Batchable**: =jax.vmap(solve_lp)= over a batch of constraint matrices
  with the same shape runs all instances in one GPU kernel. For discopt,
  this means a B&B frontier of thousands of subproblems sharing a
  constraint structure but differing in bounds can be solved in a single
  GPU call.
- **Differentiable**: MPAX's optimum can be differentiated with respect to
  the problem data — constraint matrix, right-hand side, objective — using
  implicit-function-theorem-based rules. This enables gradient-based
  hyperparameter tuning of heuristics, sensitivity analysis, and
  parameter-estimation pipelines that embed an LP.

** Strengths and Weaknesses Relative to HiGHS

| Dimension | HiGHS | MPAX |
|---------------------+--------------+-------|
| Accuracy | $10^{-9}$+ | $10^{-4}$ to $10^{-6}$ |
| Small LP performance | Fast (simplex) | 5-50x slower |
| Large LP performance | Hard to scale past 1M nonzeros | Scales to $10^8$ on GPU |
| Warm-start quality | Excellent (basis) | Poor (iterate-level only) |
| Batchable | No | Yes (=vmap=) |
| Differentiable | No | Yes |
| Language | C++ | Python/JAX |
| License | MIT | MIT |

The implication is that MPAX is **not** a replacement for HiGHS — it is a
complement. For small, highly-accurate LPs that arise in B&B bound
evaluation, HiGHS simplex remains the correct choice. MPAX takes over in
the regime where LPs are large (hundreds of thousands of nonzeros or more),
where batching across many LP instances gives GPU acceleration, or where
downstream computation needs gradients through the LP solve.

** Role in discopt

*** Short-term: MPAX as an opt-in LP backend

Wrap MPAX behind the same interface as HiGHS in =python/discopt/solvers/=.
Add =MPAXBackend= alongside =HiGHSBackend=. Make the choice user-selectable
via a solver parameter. Benchmark on the existing smoke and phase1 suites
to determine the crossover point (problem size above which MPAX wins on
wall-clock). The HiGHS backend remains the default until that crossover is
characterized.

*** Medium-term: MPAX inside GPU-batched heuristics

Once MPAX is wrapped, use it as the LP projection step inside:

- The [[file:../methods/feasibility-pump.org][feasibility pump]] — see the PDLP-pump discussion in
  [[file:../methods/first-order-mip-heuristics.org][first-order MIP heuristics]].
- Batched random rounding with LP feasibility checks.
- Matheuristic sub-problems where the inner LP is large but approximate
  solutions suffice.

MPAX's =vmap= support means a single GPU call can solve a whole portfolio
of LP sub-problems — the equivalent operation on HiGHS would be
sequential solves with Python loop overhead.

*** Long-term: Differentiable-LP-enabled features

The unique capability of MPAX is differentiability. Examples of long-term
features that become feasible only with a differentiable LP backend:

- **Gradient-based cut-selection policy**: learn which cuts to add at each
  node by gradient descent on end-to-end solve time, with gradients
  propagated through the LP relaxations each cut affects.
- **Parameter estimation with embedded LP**: when the objective or
  constraints depend on unknown parameters, fit the parameters by back-
  propagating from the LP solution through MPAX to the parameters.
- **Design-of-experiments with LP-embedded utility**: propose experiments
  that maximize the /derivative/ of the LP optimum with respect to
  parameters.

These are research directions rather than near-term deliverables, but they
illustrate why MPAX is a strategic choice, not merely a performance one.

** License and Integration

MPAX is MIT-licensed. Integration into discopt is straightforward: install
as a dependency (optional, via =discopt[mpax]=), import in
=python/discopt/solvers/mpax.py=, expose the same =solve_lp= interface as
the HiGHS wrapper. No proprietary components, no build-system surprises.
The main engineering effort is in the batching and differentiability
layers, which are discopt-specific.

** Relationship to HiGHS's cuPDLP Path

HiGHS 1.7+ vendors cuPDLP-C in =highs/pdlp/cupdlp/= and exposes it through
the standard HiGHS LP API. Users of HiGHS can already get PDLP-style
performance on GPUs. However, HiGHS's cuPDLP integration does not expose
=vmap=, differentiability, or JAX composition. For a CPU-only or
performance-only integration, HiGHS's cuPDLP is sufficient. For discopt's
long-term plans to exploit differentiability and GPU batching in
heuristics, MPAX is the correct choice.
