Mixed-Integer Nonlinear Programming (MINLP) combines the combinatorial difficulty
of integer variables with the analytical complexity of nonlinear functions. MINLPs
arise in process synthesis, network design, scheduling, engineering design, and
increasingly in machine learning (neural network verification, optimization over
trained models).

** Problem Classification

MINLPs are classified along two axes. The first is convexity: convex MINLPs have
convex objective and constraint functions in the continuous variables, and can be
solved to global optimality by decomposition methods (OA, GBD) or NLP-BB.
Nonconvex MINLPs require spatial branch and bound with convex relaxations for
global optimality guarantees citep:grossmann2002.

The second axis is the type of nonlinearity: quadratic (QP, MIQP), polynomial,
signomial (sums of products of powers), or general nonlinear (transcendental
functions like exp, log, sin). The type of nonlinearity determines which
relaxation techniques are most effective citep:belotti2013.

** Algorithmic Landscape

citet:belotti2013 provides the definitive survey of MINLP methods, covering:
convex MINLP algorithms (outer approximation, generalized Benders, branch and
bound, extended cutting plane), nonconvex global optimization (spatial B&B,
convex relaxations, bound tightening), and computational aspects (software,
benchmarks, modeling). citet:grossmann2002 provides the unified theoretical
framework relating OA, GBD, ECP, and B&B, showing the dominance hierarchy
OA >= GBD >= LP relaxation for convex MINLPs.

** discopt's Position

discopt is a hybrid MINLP solver targeting both convex and nonconvex problems.
For convex MINLPs, it uses NLP-BB with batched JAX IPM solving. For nonconvex
MINLPs, it uses spatial B&B with McCormick relaxations and alphaBB
underestimators. It benchmarks against BARON (global optimization gold standard),
SCIP (open-source spatial B&B), and Couenne (COIN-OR global optimizer).
