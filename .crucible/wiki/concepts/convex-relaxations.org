* Convex Relaxations for Global Optimization
:PROPERTIES:
:ARTICLE_TYPE: concept
:SOURCE_KEYS: tawarmalani2002 sherali1990 liberti2003
:STATUS: draft
:CREATED: 2026-04-04
:END:

Convex relaxations are the theoretical foundation of deterministic global
optimization. A convex relaxation of a nonconvex optimization problem replaces
the nonconvex feasible set and/or objective with convex approximations that
contain the original feasible set (outer approximation) and underestimate the
original objective. The optimal value of the relaxation provides a valid lower
bound on the original problem's global optimum.

** Convex Envelopes

The convex envelope of a function f over a domain D is the tightest convex
underestimator: the pointwise supremum of all affine functions that underestimate
f on D. Computing convex envelopes analytically is possible for several important
function classes citep:tawarmalani2002, including bilinear terms (McCormick envelope),
univariate convex/concave functions (trivial: the function itself or the secant),
and univariate monomials of any degree citep:liberti2003. For general nonlinear
functions, the convex envelope is typically not available in closed form, and
constructive relaxation methods (McCormick, alphaBB) provide valid but potentially
non-tight underestimators.

** Reformulation-Linearization Technique (RLT)

RLT citep:sherali1990 constructs a hierarchy of progressively tighter LP
relaxations for problems with polynomial and multiplicative structure. At level
d, RLT multiplies each constraint by all products of d bound factors (x_i -
x_i^L and x_i^U - x_i) and linearizes the resulting polynomial terms by
introducing new variables. Level 1 RLT for bilinear terms produces the
McCormick envelope. Higher levels produce tighter relaxations at the cost of
exponentially more variables and constraints. BARON uses RLT internally to
strengthen its LP relaxations.

** Hierarchy of Relaxation Methods

The main relaxation approaches for nonconvex MINLP, ordered roughly from
tightest to loosest, are: convex envelopes (when available, exact) > McCormick
relaxations (compositional, DAG-based) > alphaBB underestimators (Hessian-based)
> LP relaxation (linear constraints only). In practice, solvers combine multiple
techniques: BARON uses convex envelopes, RLT, and McCormick; Couenne uses
McCormick and cutting planes; discopt uses McCormick relaxations and alphaBB
underestimators.
