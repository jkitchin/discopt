BARON (Branch-And-Reduce Optimization Navigator) is the leading commercial
solver for deterministic global optimization of nonconvex mixed-integer nonlinear
programs (MINLPs) citep:sahinidis1996. First released in the mid-1990s, it
remains the gold standard against which newer global solvers are measured. In the
discopt benchmarking framework, BARON serves as the primary reference solver for
correctness verification and performance comparison on nonconvex MINLP instances.

** Branch-and-Reduce Framework

BARON implements the branch-and-reduce algorithm, which augments classical
spatial branch and bound with a "reduce" phase at every node
citep:ryoo1996. During the reduce phase, the solver applies range reduction
techniques, including feasibility-based bounds tightening (FBBT), optimality-based
bounds tightening (OBBT), and probing, to contract variable domains before
branching. This aggressive domain reduction is critical because tighter variable
bounds feed directly into tighter [[file:convex-relaxations.org][convex relaxations]], creating a virtuous
cycle that accelerates convergence. The combination of branching and reduction
gives BARON its name and distinguishes it from pure branch-and-bound approaches.
For a broader discussion of range reduction techniques see [[file:../methods/bound-tightening.org][bound tightening]].

** Factorable Relaxation and RLT

BARON constructs convex relaxations automatically by exploiting the factorable
structure of nonlinear programs citep:mccormick1976. Every factorable function
can be decomposed into a sequence of elementary operations (addition,
multiplication, division, power, logarithm, exponential, trigonometric functions),
and BARON introduces auxiliary variables for each intermediate expression. For
bilinear and multilinear terms, the solver generates [[file:../methods/mccormick-relaxations.org][McCormick envelopes]]; for
univariate functions, it constructs convex and concave envelopes
citep:tawarmalani2002a. The Reformulation-Linearization Technique (RLT) of
Sherali and Adams citep:sherali1990 provides additional linear relaxation
constraints for polynomial terms by multiplying bound factors and linearizing the
resulting products. These RLT cuts, combined with McCormick relaxations, yield LP
relaxations whose solutions provide rigorous lower bounds at each node of the
branch-and-bound tree.

Tawarmalani and Sahinidis citep:tawarmalani2004 significantly advanced this
relaxation framework by developing tighter convex underestimators for fractional,
bilinear, and concave terms. Their computational study demonstrated that the
combined effect of tight relaxations and aggressive domain reduction enabled the
solution of challenging MINLPs that were previously intractable, marking a major
milestone in BARON's development. The monograph citep:tawarmalani2002b provides
the comprehensive theoretical foundation for BARON's convexification machinery.

** Polyhedral Branch-and-Cut

A distinctive feature of BARON's relaxation strategy is the polyhedral
branch-and-cut approach introduced by Tawarmalani and Sahinidis
citep:tawarmalani2005. The key insight is that when a nonlinear function can be
decomposed into convex subexpressions (either identified automatically through
convexity detection or supplied via modeling constructs), the relaxation
constructor can exploit this convexity in a manner provably superior to
developing polyhedral outer approximators for the original function. The approach
generates supporting hyperplanes of the convex relaxation at solutions of the LP
relaxation, progressively tightening the polyhedral approximation. This hybrid
LP/NLP relaxation strategy balances the tightness of nonlinear relaxations with
the computational efficiency of LP solving, and it became a central component of
BARON's toolkit for handling problems with identifiable convex structure.

** Exploiting Integrality

Traditional spatial branch-and-bound solvers treated integer variables similarly
to continuous ones, a historical weakness that Kilinc and Sahinidis
citep:kilinc2018 addressed by introducing techniques that exploit integrality
within BARON. Their primary contribution is the addition of mixed-integer linear
programming (MILP) relaxations to BARON's portfolio of LP and NLP relaxations.
By constructing MILP relaxations that respect the integrality of discrete
variables, BARON obtains significantly tighter dual bounds and better starting
points for primal heuristics. The paper also introduces improved branching
strategies and domain reduction techniques specifically designed for binary and
general integer variables. Computational experiments on the MINLPLib benchmark
library demonstrated substantial speedups, particularly on problems with
significant integer structure, bringing BARON's MINLP capabilities closer to the
sophisticated integer programming techniques found in dedicated MILP solvers.

** Role in discopt Benchmarking

BARON is the primary benchmark competitor for discopt. The benchmarking framework
(=discopt_benchmarks/=) uses BARON as the reference solver for correctness
verification, comparing optimal objective values and solution quality across
nonconvex MINLP test problems. Performance profiles and phase gate criteria
measure discopt's progress relative to BARON's solve times and success rates.
Understanding BARON's algorithmic machinery, from its [[file:convex-relaxations.org][convex relaxations]] and RLT
cuts to its [[file:../methods/bound-tightening.org][bound tightening]] strategies, informs the design decisions in
discopt's own [[file:../methods/spatial-branch-and-bound.org][spatial branch-and-bound]] implementation.
