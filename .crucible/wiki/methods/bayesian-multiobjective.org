* Bayesian Multiobjective Optimization
:PROPERTIES:
:ARTICLE_TYPE: method
:SOURCE_KEYS: knowles-2006 emmerich-deutz-2018
:STATUS: draft
:CREATED: 2026-04-24
:END:

Bayesian (surrogate-assisted) multiobjective optimization targets
/expensive/ MOPs --- each f_i(x) evaluation requires a simulation,
physical experiment, or long-running computation, so evaluation budgets
are on the order of 10--1000. The core idea is to fit a probabilistic
surrogate (typically a Gaussian process per objective) and use an
acquisition function to pick the next evaluation that maximizes expected
progress on the Pareto front.

** ParEGO

citet:knowles-2006 introduced ParEGO, the first widely adopted surrogate
MOEA. Each iteration:

1. Sample a random weight vector w from the simplex.
2. Scalarize the observed evaluations via an augmented weighted
   Tchebycheff into a single surrogate dataset.
3. Fit a single GP to the scalarized values.
4. Select the next point by maximizing Expected Improvement (EI) on this
   GP.

ParEGO's elegance is that it reuses single-objective EGO/Kriging
machinery unchanged; its weakness is that the random scalarization
discards information across iterations and requires a fresh surrogate
fit per iteration.

** Expected Hypervolume Improvement (EHVI)

Modern Bayesian multi-objective methods fit one GP per objective and use
the /Expected Hypervolume Improvement/ acquisition function: the expected
increase in [[file:../concepts/performance-indicators-mo.org][hypervolume]] dominated by the current Pareto front if a new
point is observed at x, taken under the joint posterior of the GPs
citep:emmerich-deutz-2018.

EHVI variants:

- /qEHVI/: batch (parallel) EHVI using inclusion-exclusion over the
  nondominated partitioning of objective space. Widely implemented in
  BoTorch.
- /qNEHVI/: noisy qEHVI, robust to GP observation noise via
  Monte-Carlo integration over the posterior; replaces qEHVI as the
  recommended default in noisy problems.
- /MOBO-EHVI/ with trust regions: handles high-dimensional search
  spaces (e.g., TuRBO-based variants) where global GP fits degrade.

Hypervolume-based acquisition is Pareto-compliant by construction:
maximizing expected hypervolume gain drives the front toward
Pareto-optimality in the limit of infinite evaluations
citep:emmerich-deutz-2018.

** Practical Use

Typical application domains are chemistry/materials ("design the
molecule", "choose the synthesis parameters"), engineering design
("minimize mass and maximize stiffness"), and autoML ("pick
hyperparameters trading off accuracy and inference time"). Tools in
current use:

- *BoTorch* + *Ax* (PyTorch-based): qEHVI, qNEHVI, parallel batching,
  discrete/mixed spaces.
- *Trieste* (TensorFlow): EHVI and decomposition-based acquisitions.
- *scikit-optimize*, *GPyOpt*: older ParEGO/EHVI implementations.

** Relevance for discopt

Bayesian multi-objective optimization is complementary to discopt's
deterministic MINLP focus:

- discopt can evaluate or bound f_i(x) exactly when f_i is given
  algebraically --- no surrogate needed. Bayesian methods matter when f_i
  is a simulation or experiment.
- A Bayesian outer loop could /choose experiments/ for parameters of a
  discopt-solved inner problem, using discopt for the inner MINLP and a
  GP surrogate for the outer design variables. This is the common
  pattern in model-based [[file:../concepts/model-based-doe.org][design of experiments]] and would be a natural
  integration point for a future discopt.mo module.
