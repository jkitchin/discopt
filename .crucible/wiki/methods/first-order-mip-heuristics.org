Neither [[file:../concepts/scip-solver.org][SCIP]] nor [[file:../concepts/highs-solver.org][HiGHS]] has a first-class first-order LP backend
integrated with their MIP primal heuristics. HiGHS added a PDLP path
(=highs/pdlp/=) in version 1.7 but uses it as an alternative LP
solver, not as the backbone of a GPU-batched heuristic portfolio. The
post-2020 first-order LP literature — PDLP, cuPDLP, MPAX — is
architecturally very well suited to hosting MIP primal heuristics on a GPU,
and combining it with NVIDIA Warp kernels (see [[file:feasibility-jump.org][feasibility jump]] and
[[file:mip-presolve.org][MIP presolve]]) opens an entire class of heuristics that CPU-only solvers
cannot easily match. This article surveys the landscape and records
discopt's plan to take advantage.

** Background: First-Order LP Solvers

Interior-point and simplex LP solvers dominate small-to-medium LPs but
scale poorly to $10^7$+ nonzeros without sophisticated parallelism. First-
order methods — gradient-style iterations that require only matrix-vector
products — were long considered too slow and insufficiently accurate for
practical LP, until citet:applegate-pdlp-2021 showed that a carefully
engineered primal-dual hybrid gradient (PDHG) algorithm with restart
heuristics, adaptive step sizes, and infeasibility detection could solve
very large LPs in wall-clock time comparable to simplex on single
machines, with much better scalability to GPUs.

- **PDLP** (citet:applegate-pdlp-2021, NeurIPS 2021) is Google's
  restart-and-scale PDHG implementation. Open-source, widely benchmarked.
- **cuPDLP** (citet:lu-cupdlp-2023) is MIT-Lu-Lab's GPU-native
  reimplementation of PDLP in Julia (and ported to C in HiGHS as
  =highs/pdlp/cupdlp=). cuPDLP demonstrated that a single NVIDIA A100 GPU
  can solve LPs with $10^8$+ nonzeros competitively with multi-node CPU
  simplex.
- **MPAX** (citet:mpax-2024) is a JAX-native port of PDLP by the same
  MIT-Lu-Lab team. See the paired concept article [[file:../concepts/mpax-solver.org][MPAX solver]] for depth.
  The key property for discopt: MPAX is built entirely from JAX primitives,
  so LP solves are =vmap=-able across a batch of LP instances and
  differentiable through implicit function theorems.

** Why First-Order LP Matters for MIP Heuristics

The cost profile of first-order LP is opposite to that of simplex or IPM:
per-iteration cost is cheap (one sparse matrix-vector product), but many
iterations may be needed and the final accuracy is looser ($10^{-4}$ to
$10^{-6}$ relative, not $10^{-9}$). This profile is /ideal/ for a class of
MIP primal heuristics whose inner LP solves are used for rough guidance,
not for proving optimality bounds. The prototypical example is the
[[file:feasibility-pump.org][feasibility pump]]: each iteration projects a rounded point onto the LP
polytope, and the solver does not need full accuracy — a few digits are
sufficient to guide the next rounding step. Replacing the simplex LP in the
pump with a PDLP/cuPDLP/MPAX solve drops per-iteration cost from
milliseconds to microseconds on large instances.

More importantly, first-order LP is /batchable/ on a GPU. Running 1000
feasibility-pump replicas with different random seeds in parallel is a
single GPU kernel launch with MPAX, rather than 1000 sequential simplex
solves. This matches the architectural pattern established for
[[file:feasibility-jump.org][feasibility jump]] and for batched frontier propagation (see
[[file:mip-presolve.org][MIP presolve]] Warp opportunities).

** Concrete Heuristic Patterns

*** PDLP/MPAX-Accelerated Feasibility Pump

Replace the LP projection step in the [[file:feasibility-pump.org][feasibility pump]] with a
PDLP or MPAX solve. On large MILPs, the pump throughput increases by
orders of magnitude because each LP is now a first-order solve on a
structure-rich problem rather than a full simplex re-solve. In JAX, the
pump becomes =vmap(single_pump_step)= over a batch of initial rounded
points, with each element of the batch running its own restart chain.
**Host: Python driver + JAX/MPAX inner solver.**

*** Random Rounding Portfolio on GPU

Classical randomized rounding runs one seed at a time. A JAX/MPAX or Warp
kernel can run thousands of seeds in parallel, score each by constraint
violation after rounding, and return the best. This is cheap per-seed and
the parallel score sweep is embarrassingly vectorizable. **Host: JAX**
(dense path) or **Warp** (scatter/atomic path).

*** GPU-Batched Feasibility Jump

Already discussed in [[file:feasibility-jump.org][feasibility jump]]. PDLP/MPAX is not required (FJ is
LP-free), but batching FJ replicas across GPU threads is in the same
architectural lane. Combining batched FJ with MPAX-warmstarted LP
projections gives a hybrid feasibility engine.

*** ADMM Feasibility Heuristic for MIQP

citet:takapoui-admm-mip-2017 proposed an ADMM-based primal heuristic for
mixed-integer quadratic programs. The ADMM splits the problem into a
continuous convex subproblem (solvable in closed form or via a small QP)
and an integer rounding subproblem (one-shot projection onto the integer
set). The iteration is a one-line update that parallelizes trivially. For
discopt's MIQP + convex-MINLP workloads, this is a natural JAX/Warp port
target. **Host: JAX** or **Warp** (batch over variable domains).

*** Differentiable LP in Heuristic Design

MPAX's differentiability enables heuristic designs that are out of reach
for simplex-based solvers. Examples:

- **Learned LP cut selection**: gradient-descend the cut-selection policy
  against time-to-improvement, with gradients flowing through an MPAX LP
  relaxation.
- **Parameter estimation with embedded LP**: discopt's parameter-
  estimation layer can back-propagate through LP constraints (parameter
  → LP → solution → loss), making a class of inverse-problem heuristics
  feasible.
- **Differentiable feasibility pump**: use gradient information from the
  MPAX LP to guide the rounding direction, not just the projection value.

These are research directions rather than short-term ports, but they
illustrate why MPAX is strategically important beyond raw throughput.

*** cuOpt (NVIDIA)

NVIDIA's cuOpt MIP stack (closed source; documentation only) includes
GPU-batched primal heuristics that share the scatter-kernel pattern
discussed here and in the Warp discussion in [[file:feasibility-jump.org][feasibility jump]]. No peer-
reviewed paper exists at the time of writing; citations are to the
NVIDIA documentation. cuOpt serves as a commercial proof-of-concept that
GPU-batched MIP heuristics are viable at scale; discopt's open-source
JAX+Warp implementation targets the same design space with a different
license story and a more research-friendly architecture.

** Host-Placement Summary

| Component | Host | Rationale |
|----------------------------+-------+-----------|
| MPAX-pumped feasibility pump | JAX + Python | Inner LP is JAX; driver is Python. |
| Batched random rounding | JAX or Warp | Dense SIMD vs. scatter/atomic tradeoff. |
| Batched Feasibility Jump | JAX or Warp | See [[file:feasibility-jump.org][FJ article]]. |
| ADMM-MIQP heuristic | JAX or Warp | Closed-form updates vectorize. |
| Differentiable-LP heuristics | JAX + MPAX | Requires gradient through LP. |
| cuOpt-style batched LNS | Warp | Sub-MIP-per-thread not yet viable; for rounding-family only. |

OBBT orchestration (one LP per tightened bound) can use MPAX when the LP
is large, simplex when the LP is small. The dispatcher lives in Python.

** Relationship to GitHub Issue #37

Issue #37 on =jkitchin/discopt= asks for MPAX integration and for porting
SCIP/HiGHS heuristics to Rust. This article records the /second/ half of
the answer: once MPAX is integrated, an entire category of heuristics
becomes viable that is not reachable from Rust alone. The port plan
records MPAX integration as Short-term, and the MPAX-based heuristic
family (PDLP-pump, differentiable-LP heuristics, cuOpt-style batched
rounding) as Medium-to-Long term.

** Role in discopt

First-order and GPU heuristics are the /other/ half of discopt's
architectural differentiation against SCIP and HiGHS. While
[[file:learning-based-mip-heuristics.org][learning-based MIP heuristics]] exploits discopt's ML/LLM layer, this
family exploits its JAX numerics layer and the associated GPU
batching/differentiability. The two families are complementary: learned
heuristics choose /what/ to do, first-order heuristics provide the /fast
primitives/ that make many of those choices cheap to try.

The short-term win is MPAX integration for the existing feasibility pump.
The medium-term win is a batched GPU feasibility engine (FJ + random
rounding + ADMM-MIQP) running as a Python-orchestrated pool over a JAX or
Warp kernel. The long-term win is differentiable-LP-enabled heuristics
that SCIP and HiGHS cannot implement without rewriting their LP backends.
