* Auxiliary Variable Reformulation
:PROPERTIES:
:ARTICLE_TYPE: method
:SOURCE_KEYS: smith-pantelides-1999 belotti2009 cafieri2010 tawarmalani-sahinidis-2002
:STATUS: draft
:CREATED: 2026-04-16

Auxiliary-variable reformulation is the preprocessing step that turns a
nonconvex factorable MINLP into an equivalent system where every nonlinear
operation is isolated in its own atomic relation. It is the foundation on
which [[file:mccormick-relaxations.org][McCormick relaxations]], spatial
branching, and modern global-MINLP solvers are built.

** Construction

Given an expression DAG built from factorable operations
(addition, multiplication, univariate functions), walk the DAG bottom-up.
For every intermediate node /v = op(u_1, …, u_k)/ that is not already a
leaf or an affine combination, introduce a fresh auxiliary variable
/w_v/ bounded by propagated interval arithmetic, and replace the node by
the identity /w_v = op(u_1, …, u_k)/. The resulting system has:

- Linear variables: the original decision variables plus all auxiliaries.
- Linear constraints: the original linear constraints, rewritten in terms
  of auxiliaries.
- A list of /simple/ nonconvex atoms /w_v = op(…)/ -- at most one
  bilinear product, square, quotient, or univariate nonlinearity per row.

This form is variously called "factorable reformulation"
citep:smith-pantelides-1999, "auxiliary-variable method," or
(in citep:tawarmalani-sahinidis-2002) the BARON reformulation. Couenne
and BARON both use it; citet:belotti2009 describes Couenne's specific
expression DAG and how auxiliaries are allocated.

** Why it matters

- Each atom /w = op(u)/ is simple enough that its convex and concave
  envelopes are either known in closed form (McCormick for bilinear,
  secant underestimators for convex univariates, etc.) or can be
  generated by tabulated rules.
- The LP relaxation is a function of only these atomic envelopes plus the
  original linear constraints, so the relaxation constructor is bounded
  in complexity regardless of how deeply the original expression is
  nested.
- Spatial branching becomes a decision over a /flat/ set of variables
  (the originals plus auxiliaries), not over arbitrary DAG nodes, which
  simplifies branching rules and bound propagation.

** Multilinear atoms

For multilinear products /x_1 x_2 … x_k/ with k ≥ 3, the standard
approach factors the product as successive bilinear products
(/w_12 = x_1 x_2/, /w_123 = w_12 x_3/, …). citet:cafieri2010 prove that
this is not optimal: replacing one of the bilinear factorings with a
/trilinear/ factoring produces a tighter relaxation, because the convex
envelope of a trilinear term on a box is known exactly whereas the
envelope of a product of two bilinears is not. Couenne therefore prefers
trilinear atoms when allocating auxiliaries for multilinear products.

** Relationship to other methods

- [[file:mccormick-relaxations.org][McCormick relaxations]] are applied
  to each atom after reformulation.
- [[file:bound-tightening.org][FBBT and OBBT]] operate on the expanded
  variable set; the tighter the bounds on the auxiliaries, the tighter
  the envelopes.
- [[file:spatial-branch-and-bound.org][Spatial branch-and-bound]]
  selects the next auxiliary/original variable on which to split the
  domain, guided by violation-transfer heuristics that measure how much
  each atom's envelope gap is contributing to the relaxation gap.
- [[file:disjunctive-cuts-minlp.org][Disjunctive cuts]] are generated over
  the polyhedron defined by the linear constraints plus the atomic
  envelopes.
