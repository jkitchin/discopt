* Disjunctive Cuts for Nonconvex MINLP
:PROPERTIES:
:ARTICLE_TYPE: method
:SOURCE_KEYS: belotti2012 belotti2009
:STATUS: draft
:CREATED: 2026-04-16
:END:

Disjunctive cuts are valid linear inequalities derived from a disjunction
(a union of polyhedra) that the feasible region must satisfy. In MILP,
generating disjunctive cuts from a simple /x_i ≤ 0 OR x_i ≥ 1/ disjunction
via Balas's cut-generating LP (CGLP) is a classical technique that unified
Gomory, lift-and-project, and intersection cuts. citet:belotti2012 adapts
this machinery to nonconvex MINLP, targeting disjunctions that arise from
spatial branching (x ≤ m OR x ≥ m, where m is some interior split point of a
continuous variable) as well as from integrality.

** Setting

After auxiliary-variable reformulation (see [[file:auxiliary-variable-reformulation.org][auxiliary variable reformulation]]), the feasible region of a
nonconvex MINLP can be written as the intersection of a polyhedron P (from
McCormick envelopes and the linear constraints) with a set of nonconvex
"simple" relations (/w = x y/, /w = x^2/, /w = exp(x)/, etc.). At a given
node, one has a fractional or relaxation-infeasible LP solution x* and a
branching disjunction /D_1 ∨ D_2/ induced by either an integer variable or
a spatial split on a continuous one.

** Cut-generating LP

Let P_1 = P ∩ D_1 and P_2 = P ∩ D_2. Any inequality /α^T x ≤ β/ valid for
both P_1 and P_2 is valid for the MINLP. citet:belotti2012 derives an
explicit CGLP that, for a given x*, computes the deepest such cut
(largest α^T x* − β) over a normalization cone. Because the auxiliary
reformulation keeps each nonconvex atom bounded by its McCormick envelope,
the CGLP remains linear even though the underlying problem is nonconvex.

The resulting cut is added to the LP relaxation at the current node (and
optionally inherited by descendants), tightening the outer approximation.

** When it helps

- At the root, a few disjunctive cuts lifted from the strongest integer
  fractional variable, or from the widest-gap continuous variable, can
  close a meaningful fraction of the root gap.
- At deeper nodes, the technique is more useful on problems where McCormick
  envelopes are slack and where spatial branching alone converges slowly.
- As in MILP, generating too many disjunctive cuts is counterproductive,
  because each additional cut inflates the LP basis; Couenne caps the cut
  pool and evicts low-efficacy cuts.

** Relationship to other Couenne components

- Complements [[file:bound-tightening.org][FBBT and OBBT]] by providing /globally/ valid inequalities
  that bound-tightening cannot produce.
- Uses the same auxiliary-variable layer as [[file:mccormick-relaxations.org][McCormick relaxations]].
- Generated cuts are passed to the Cgl cut manager (from COIN-OR) and
  mixed with standard MILP cuts at each node.
