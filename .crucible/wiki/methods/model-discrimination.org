Model discrimination is the twin problem of parameter estimation: rather
than refining parameters of a single model, it designs experiments that
maximally distinguish between several rival model structures and then
picks the best post-experiment. Both halves are classical in chemical
engineering (kinetic mechanism discrimination) and are supported in
~discopt.doe~ through the ~discrimination~, ~discrimination_sequential~,
and ~selection~ submodules.

** Problem Statement

Given M candidate models {M_i} each with parameter vector theta_i fitted
to initial data, an experimental-design question asks: /what next
experiment d will most separate the model predictions?/ Post-experiment,
a selection question asks: /which model best fits the data?/ Both are
answered in *prediction space* so the candidates need not share parameter
names or counts citep:buzzi-ferraris-1983.

For each model i, discopt evaluates the prediction
  y_i(d) = f_i(theta_hat_i, d)
and its prediction covariance
  V_i(d) = J_i(d) * FIM_i^{-1} * J_i(d)^T
where J_i is the Jacobian of f_i with respect to theta_i at the current
estimate. Discrimination criteria are functions of {y_i, V_i}.

** Five Discrimination Criteria (Pre-Experiment)

*** Hunter-Reiner (HR, 1965)

The original bi-model criterion citep:hunter-reiner-1965:

  HR(d) = (y_1(d) - y_2(d))^T * Sigma_y^{-1} * (y_1(d) - y_2(d))

where Sigma_y is the measurement-noise covariance. Maximize HR to find
the design where the two predictions differ the most (noise-weighted).
*Ignores prediction uncertainty*, so can propose designs in regions where
both predictions are poorly known.

*** Buzzi-Ferraris-Forzatti (BF, 1983) --- discopt's default

Multiresponse, handles M >= 2, normalizes by both measurement noise and
prediction-variance covariances citep:buzzi-ferraris-1983:

  BF(d) = sum_{i<j} (y_i - y_j)^T * (V_i + V_j + 2*Sigma_y)^{-1} * (y_i - y_j).

The V_i + V_j term downweights designs where predictions *look* different
only because both models are uncertain there. The factor 2*Sigma_y
accounts for the measurement noise on the observed response. This is
discopt's default for its multi-model robustness and theoretical
cleanness.

*** Jensen-Rényi divergence (JR, Olofsson et al. 2019)

A symmetric, information-theoretic divergence on the per-model Gaussian
predictives N(y_i, V_i + Sigma_y) citep:olofsson-2019. Satisfies the
triangle-inequality-like properties expected of a metric and scales
naturally to M >= 2 models. Preferred over HR/BF when the predictive
distributions are strongly non-overlapping.

*** Mutual information (MI, Lindley 1956)

The Bayesian-optimal information gain about the model index M given a
future observation y at design d:

  MI(d) = I(M; y | d) = H(M) - H(M | y, d).

Estimated by nested Monte Carlo on the per-model Gaussian predictives
(Foster et al. 2019). Computationally heaviest; theoretically tightest.
Reserved for problems where the MC cost is tolerable.

*** DT-compound (Atkinson-Bogacka-Bogacki 1998)

When you care about *both* discrimination and parameter precision of the
eventually-selected model citep:atkinson-bogacka-1998:

  DT(d) = alpha * log det(FIM_pooled(d)) + (1-alpha) * BF(d)

The weight alpha tunes between D-optimal precision and BF
discrimination. Typical use: continue refining the surviving model once
discrimination is near-decided.

** Sequential Discrimination

Closed-loop version:

  1. Fit every model on initial data to get theta_hat_i and FIM_i.
  2. Compute model weights w_i (e.g., posterior from likelihood +
     prior, or Akaike weights).
  3. Design the next experiment maximizing the chosen discrimination
     criterion (optionally weight-aware).
  4. Run it, update {theta_hat_i, FIM_i, w_i}.
  5. Stop when max w_i exceeds a threshold (e.g. 0.95) or a budget is
     spent.

discopt implements this as ~sequential_discrimination~, which mirrors
~sequential_doe~ but operates on a dictionary of experiments.

** Post-Experiment Selection

After data collection, rank the fitted models. ~discopt.doe.selection~
derives every metric in one line from the estimator's /deviance/
~D(theta_hat) = -2 log L(theta_hat)~ up to a constant under Gaussian
noise, where ~EstimationResult.objective~ stores D directly.

| Method   | Formula                                                                 |
|----------+-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| AIC      | ~2*p + D~                                                               |
| AICc     | ~AIC + 2*p*(p+1)/(n - p - 1)~                                           |
| BIC      | ~p*log(n) + D~                                                          |
| LRT G^2  | ~D_nested - D_full~ on ~df = p_full - p_nested~ (requires nesting)      |
| Vuong z  | Per-observation log-likelihood ratios; for non-nested pairs             |

AIC/BIC trade off differently: BIC penalizes complexity harder (log n
vs. 2), so favors simpler models as n grows. AICc is the small-sample
correction and is usually the tie-breaker for n < 40. The LRT is exact
for nested pairs under chi-square asymptotics; Vuong is the non-nested
analog citep:vuong-1989.

Akaike weights
  w_i = exp(-0.5*(AIC_i - AIC_min)) / sum_j exp(-0.5*(AIC_j - AIC_min))
give a probabilistic interpretation of the ranking that sequential
discrimination uses as model weights in step 2.

** Connection to Other Tools

Discrimination feeds into [[file:../concepts/model-based-doe.org][model-based DoE]] when the question is which
experiment to run next; it feeds out of [[file:parameter-estimability.org][parameter estimability]] and
[[file:algebraic-model-identifiability.org][algebraic identifiability]] when the question is whether individual
models can even be fit. The /prediction-space/ operating regime means
discrimination works on models with completely different parameterizations
--- the information lives in the outputs, not the theta coordinates.

** Relevance for discopt

- Pre-experiment: ~discriminate_design(experiments, param_estimates,
  design_bounds, criterion)~ returns a ~DiscriminationDesignResult~ with
  the optimal design, per-model FIMs, and predictions.
- Sequential: ~sequential_discrimination(...)~ returns a history of
  ~DiscriminationRound~ objects and supports arbitrary run-experiment
  callbacks.
- Post-experiment: ~model_selection({"name": EstimationResult, ...},
  method="aic")~ returns a ~ModelSelectionResult~ with scores, weights,
  and the best model. ~likelihood_ratio_test~ and ~vuong_test~ are
  provided for nested and non-nested pairwise comparisons.

All five criteria are exposed via the ~DiscriminationCriterion~ enum;
BF is the default.
