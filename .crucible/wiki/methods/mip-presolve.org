Presolve, as implemented in [[file:../concepts/scip-solver.org][SCIP]] and [[file:../concepts/highs-solver.org][HiGHS]], is the fixpoint application of
a battery of /reductions/ — each a
sound simplification of the MIP — executed before branch-and-cut begins.
Reductions eliminate redundant variables and constraints, tighten
coefficients, fix variables by dual or primal dominance, substitute
implied-free variables, extract cliques and implications, and detect
symmetry. Modern commercial solvers attribute an order-of-magnitude speedup
to presolve: the 2020 survey by citet:achterberg2020presolve reports that
disabling presolve on MIPLIB benchmarks slows a state-of-the-art solver by a
shifted geometric mean of **10.7×**, and presolve typically removes
40-60% of rows, columns, and nonzeros. Because presolve runs once at the
root (and then in more limited form during tree search), its performance
benefit comes from /catching every applicable reduction/, not from inner-loop
speed — which is why the SCIP portfolio contains more than twenty distinct
reductions.

** The Canonical Survey

citet:achterberg2020presolve, drawing on decades of CPLEX and Gurobi
development, is the reference work. It catalogs the reductions, explains why
presolve is iterated to fixpoint (each reduction can enable others),
analyzes the /dual validity/ conditions under which a reduction may cut off
optimal solutions, and reports empirical findings on MIPLIB 2010/2017.
Novel contributions include two-row bound tightening (which combines two
constraints via FBBT to derive bounds no single constraint can produce) and
conic-dual reductions. The paper also carefully distinguishes *primal*
reductions (which preserve every feasible solution) from *dual* reductions
(which may discard feasible but suboptimal solutions based on objective
reasoning).

** Host Placement for discopt

All MIP presolve reductions are **Suggested host: Rust**. They sit in tight
inner loops over sparse nonzero data, run to fixpoint, and must integrate
with discopt's existing FBBT/OBBT/probing code in
=crates/discopt-core/src/presolve/=. There are two deliberate exceptions
called out below — frontier-batched FBBT and frontier-batched
McCormick/αBB coefficient recomputation — where **Warp** is the better
host because the workload shifts from "one model, tight loop" to "a
thousand subproblem models, sparse scatter with atomics." OBBT
orchestration (one LP solve per tightened bound) stays **Python** as a thin
driver calling the HiGHS backend in Rust.

** The Reductions

Paths verified against =scipopt/scip= (=master=, =src/scip/=) and
=ERGO-Code/HiGHS= (=highs/presolve/=, =highs/mip/=).

- **Singleton row/column elimination** — a row with a single nonzero
  becomes a variable bound; a column with a single nonzero in an equality
  substitutes out (free singleton) or fixes by duality. SCIP:
  =presol_trivial.c=. HiGHS: =HPresolve::singletonRow/Col=. Reference:
  citet:andersen-andersen-1995; citet:achterberg2020presolve §3.1.
- **Dual fixing** — if a column's coefficients are uniformly signed and
  the objective sign agrees, fix the variable to the appropriate bound
  without losing optimality. SCIP: =prop_dualfix.c=. Reference:
  citet:achterberg2020presolve §3.2; citet:gamrath-scip-presolve-2015.
- **Dominated columns** — column $j$ dominates $k$ when flow can shift
  from $k$ into $j$ while weakly improving the objective and respecting all
  rows; the dominated column fixes to a bound. SCIP: =presol_domcol.c=.
  HiGHS: =HPresolve::detectDominatedCol=.
- **Parallel / dominated rows** — hash-based detection of proportional row
  vectors; collapse to the tighter activity bound. SCIP: =presol_redvub.c=.
  HiGHS: =HPresolve::detectParallelRow=.
- **Coefficient tightening** — using activity bounds, coefficients of
  integer variables in inequalities can be reduced, strengthening the LP
  relaxation (Crowder-Johnson-Padberg). SCIP: =SCIPtightenCoefs= inside
  =cons_linear.c=, =cons_knapsack.c=. HiGHS:
  =HPresolve::strengthenInequalities=. Reference: citet:savelsbergh1994;
  citet:achterberg2020presolve §4.3.
- **Implied free variables / substitution** — a continuous variable with
  bounds implied by a single equality can be eliminated. SCIP:
  =presol_implint.c= and aggregation in =cons_linear.c=. HiGHS:
  =HPresolve::aggregator=.
- **Probing** — tentatively fix a binary to 0 and to 1, propagate, and
  record implications, tightened bounds, and common global fixings. SCIP:
  =prop_probing.c=. HiGHS: =HPresolve::runProbing= → =HighsImplications=.
  Reference: citet:savelsbergh1994; citet:achterberg2020presolve §7.
  Parallel two-column extension: citet:gamrath-twocol-2024.
- **Clique merging / clique table** — pairwise "at most one of
  $\{x, y\}$" implications form a conflict graph; maximal cliques become
  set-packing constraints and feed cut separation. SCIP: =sepa_clique.c=,
  =heur_clique.c=, clique table in =scip/implics.c=. HiGHS:
  =HighsCliqueTable.cpp=. Reference: Atamtürk, Nemhauser & Savelsbergh,
  /Oper. Res. Letters/ 28:31-42, 2000.
- **Implications / implied bounds** — store entailments
  $x_i = v \Rightarrow x_j \in [l', u']$ from probing or constraint
  analysis; reused by propagation and cut generation. SCIP:
  =presol_implics.c=, =prop_vbounds.c=. HiGHS: =HighsImplications.cpp=.
- **Aggregator** — Gaussian-style variable elimination using linear
  equalities, respecting integrality. SCIP: =cons_linear.c= aggregation,
  =presol_sparsify.c=. HiGHS: =HPresolve::aggregator=,
  =::equalityRowAddition=.
- **Dual substitution** — LP dual feasibility forces a variable to a
  bound or marks a constraint redundant at optimum. SCIP:
  =presol_dualinfer.c=, =presol_dualcomp.c=.
- **Stuffing / dual aggregation** — singleton or structured continuous
  variables with favorable dual sign absorb slack from adjacent rows. SCIP:
  =presol_stuffing.c=, =presol_dualagg.c=.
- **Connected-component decomposition** — if the constraint graph splits
  into independent blocks, solve each separately. SCIP: =cons_components.c=.
  HiGHS: =PresolveComponent.cpp=. (Rust detection; Python orchestrates
  independent subsolves.)
- **Two-row bound tightening** — FBBT over pairs of rows, using hashing to
  make $O(n^2)$ pair enumeration tractable. SCIP: =presol_tworowbnd.c=.
  HiGHS: two-row logic in =HPresolve.cpp=. Reference:
  citet:achterberg2020presolve §4.4; citet:gemander-2020.
- **Sparsify** — add a multiple of one row to another to cancel nonzeros,
  reducing matrix density. SCIP: =presol_sparsify.c=, =presol_dualsparsify.c=.
  HiGHS: =HPresolve::sparsify=.
- **Symmetry detection and orbital fixing** — build a colored-graph
  automorphism representation, detect orbits with bliss/nauty, fix by
  orbital branching or add orbitope/symresack constraints. SCIP:
  =symmetry.c=, =symmetry_orbital.c=, =cons_orbitope*.c=, =cons_symresack.c=.
  HiGHS: =HighsSymmetry.cpp=. Reference: Margot, "Symmetry in integer
  linear programming," in /50 Years of Integer Programming/, 2010;
  Pfetsch & Rehn, /Math. Prog. Comp./ 11:733-801, 2019. Rust with a
  bliss/nauty C-binding is the practical path.

** Warp Opportunity: Batched Frontier FBBT

Root presolve is inherently serial per instance and belongs in Rust. The
interesting GPU case is during tree search, when discopt's B&B holds a
frontier of thousands of open subproblems that share the same sparse
constraint /structure/ but differ only in their variable-bound vectors.
Running FBBT at every node is expensive serially, but is a textbook Warp
kernel: launch one thread per (subproblem × row), scatter-update bound
tightenings with atomics across subproblems, iterate to fixpoint. This is
distinct from what JAX does well — it is irregular sparse scatter with
atomics, not dense tensor algebra. Primary reference for FBBT:
citet:belotti2012 §3.4; the frontier-batched variant is novel and is
discopt's architectural win over HiGHS and SCIP. The same argument applies
to **batched McCormick / αBB coefficient recomputation** across a
frontier — each subproblem shares the operator DAG but needs fresh
envelope coefficients when bounds change; a Warp kernel launching one
thread per (node × nonlinear operator) fits natively.

** MINLP-Specific Presolve

Beyond linear reductions, nonlinear presolve adds structure detection and
bound tightening over the expression DAG — directly relevant to discopt's
JAX DAG.

- citet:vigerske2018 describes SCIP's expression DAG, =cons_nonlinear.c=,
  FBBT, OBBT via =prop_obbt.c= and =prop_nlobbt.c=, and reverse propagation
  through elementary operators.
- citet:belotti2012 /Acta Numerica/ 22:1-131 is the canonical survey:
  FBBT, OBBT, convex/concave envelopes, spatial B&B.
- citet:puranik2017 focuses on domain reduction for nonlinear problems:
  FBBT, OBBT, reduced-cost tightening, probing, duality-based tightening.
- citet:gleixner2017 three OBBT enhancements — filtering, Lagrangian
  variable bounds, and warm-starting — directly applicable to discopt's
  =obbt.rs=.

** Recent Developments (2022-2026)

- citet:papilo-2023 — solver-independent parallel presolve with
  transaction-based conflict resolution and exact-arithmetic support.
  Template for a rayon-parallelized presolve loop in discopt.
- citet:gemander-2020 — hashing scheme that makes $O(n^2)$ two-row
  presolve tractable.
- citet:gamrath-twocol-2024 — parallel two-column probing.
- citet:hoen-veripb-2024 — pseudo-Boolean proof logging (VeriPB) for
  presolve, aligning with discopt's "correctness is non-negotiable"
  invariant.
- citet:liu-l2p-2024 — ICLR 2024; supervised learning predicts which
  presolvers to run for how long on a per-instance basis. Natural fit for
  discopt's LLM/ML layer.

** Port-Order Recommendation for discopt

discopt already has FBBT, OBBT, and single-variable probing in
=crates/discopt-core/src/presolve/{fbbt,obbt,probing,simplify}.rs=. The
highest-value additions, all **Rust** unless noted:

1. **Coefficient tightening** — cheap, strong LP-bound impact.
2. **Clique table + clique merging** — foundational for binary-heavy
   MINLPs and for [[file:../concepts/big-m-formulations.org][big-M formulations]] in =python/discopt/nn/formulations/=.
3. **Dominated columns / parallel rows.**
4. **Aggregator / implied-free substitution.**
5. **Connected-component decomposition** — Rust detection, Python
   orchestrates independent subsolves.
6. **Two-row bound tightening** — once single-row FBBT is saturated.
7. *(Stretch)* **Frontier-batched FBBT in Warp** — activates when
   discopt propagates at every node, not only at root.
8. *(Stretch)* **Frontier-batched McCormick/αBB recomputation in Warp** —
   pairs with [[file:spatial-branch-and-bound.org][spatial branch and bound]] on the JAX DAG.

Symmetry detection and sparsify are valuable but heavier engineering lifts
(bliss/nauty binding, dense LA over sparse matrices) and should follow the
core reductions.
